The 2015 Nepal Earthquake

Stories, Impact, and Next Steps

What Happened?


On April 25, a 7.8 Magnitude earthquake shook central Nepal, the worst natural disaster in the region in over eighty years. The quake, combined with the aftershocks that followed, were felt as far away as China, India, and Bangledesh, and killed or injured over 30,000 people. As Nepali citizens worked to locate missing friends and family and find food, water, and shelter, many aid organizations mobilized to respond to the immediate needs of those in the affected areas. While millions of dollars in relief funding have been raised to support the ongoing rebuilding process and progress continues on the ground, thousands remain in temporary housing. Six months have passed since then and the news cycle may have moved on to cover more recent events, but the effects of the earthquake remain a central part of the lives of nearly everyone in Nepal.

What still needs to be done?

While progress has been made in the rebuilding process, there is still a long way for the recovery effort to go. Nepali citizens in the most affected areas still typically report that their needs are not being met and that the feedback they provide on how to improve their communities is not being heard. Moreover, information about the relief effort in the more remote districs of the country is almost non-existent. For this reason, continuing to sustain the long-term response across the country, even several months or years after the earthquake, will be critically important.
